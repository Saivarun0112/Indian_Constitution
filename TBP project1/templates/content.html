Learn About the Constitution

The Indian Constitution is the highest law of India. It was made on 26th November 1949 and started working on 26th January 1950. It set the rules for how India is governed.

Dr. B. R. Ambedkar is called the Father of the Indian Constitution. He helped write the Constitution to make sure all people have equal rights and are treated fairly.

The Preamble is the introduction to the Constitution. It says India is a free and democratic country where everyone will have justice, freedom, and equality.

Fundamental Rights are the basic rights given to every citizen. These include the right to equality, freedom of speech, education, and protection of life.

The Constituent Assembly was a group of leaders who wrote the Constitution. They met and discussed all parts carefully before agreeing on the final rules.
